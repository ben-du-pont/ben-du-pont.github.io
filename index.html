Semester Project: Obstacle Detection for Racing Go-karts

Developed a dynamic occupancy grid using a Particle Filter for obstacle detection in autonomous racing go-karts as part of a semester project at ETH Zurich's IDSC group. Leveraged LIDAR point cloud processing and clustering, outlier rejection and an EKF for motion compensation.

Master thesis: Online UWB Anchor Initialization for Autonomous Drone Localisation

Explored online initialization of Ultra-Wideband (UWB) anchors to enable precise navigation for autonomous drones during my Master thesis at AAU's CNS group (University of Klagenfurt, Austria). Direct supervision from Prof. Stephan Weiss. Tested the implementation on real hardware in the Dronehall to confirm the great results from the simulation setups.

Toy Car Simulator

Created a toy car simulator featuring a 4-wheel vehicle model with a Pacejka tire model to simulate the dynamics and navigate Formula Student-like tracks using PID and pure pursuit controllers. Ongoing work involves transitioning to reinforcement learning-based control.

Various Machine Learning Projects

Completed four comprehensive projects from ETH Zurich's Introduction to Machine Learning course, covering topics such as regression, classification, clustering, and neural networks. Achieved full points in all four exercises


Differential Drive Robot Intersection Navigation In Duckietown

As part of the Duckietown lecture at ETH Zurich, developed and implemented a computer vision-based algorithm for autonomous navigation on multiple "Duckiebots." The system enabled the robots to use computer vision to recognize different types of intersections, stop accurately at stop lines, make navigation decisions, and interact with other Duckiebots by interpreting indicators and yielding the right of way. The Duckiebots successfully identified the correct exit, navigated intersections, and followed road lanes to their next destination, demonstrating the power of computer vision in decision-making and path-following tasks.

Full Monocular Visual Odometry Pipeline

Implemented a visual odometry pipeline to estimate camera trajectories and reconstruct 3D landmarks. Utilized two-view geometry for initialization, KLT tracking with forward-backward verification, P3P RANSAC for robust pose estimation, and continuous landmark triangulation. Enhanced the system with local bundle adjustment to combat scale drift and a keyframe-based approach for improved robustness. Evaluated the pipeline on Parking, KITTI, and Malaga datasets with 85-95% tracking success rate, achieved high local trajectory consistency even during complex camera motions.


State Estimation Filters

Developed an extended Kalman filter and a particle filter to estimate a car's state using data from intermittent sensors, as part of an exercise in the Recursive Estimation course at ETH Zurich.

Reinforcement Learning for Controlling a Formula Student racecar (Ongoing)

Integrated the vehicle dynamics model from the AMZ Racing team's simulator into the Toy Car Simulator. Trained a Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) MLP model to control the car and navigate through tracks, as part of ongoing work to enhance control systems using reinforcement learning techniques.
